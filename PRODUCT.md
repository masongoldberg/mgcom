# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Canidex is for a casual, mobile-first user identifying dog breeds in the moment, usually from a live sighting, a quick photo, or a saved camera roll image. They are not using a professional field guide. They want a fast, lightweight, rewarding collection flow that feels playful without becoming confusing or toy-like.

## Product Purpose
Canidex helps users identify dog breeds, unlock collectible entries, and keep lightweight sighting records with a photo, dog name, and notes. Success means the app feels immediately understandable, quick to use on a phone, satisfying to collect in, and trustworthy enough that users will keep logging sightings over time.

## Brand Personality
Playful, clear, collected.

The tone should feel like a product interface wearing a game shell: confident, lightweight, and rewarding, without becoming noisy, childish, or fake-retro. The user should feel curious and rewarded, but never slowed down by the aesthetic.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS dashboard polish with feature-card overload
- Overly realistic game UI with fantasy chrome, lore-heavy copy, or cluttered HUD framing
- Loud arcade energy, novelty interfaces, or screens that feel like toys instead of usable tools
- Modal-heavy flows that feel disconnected from the page and interrupt task completion

## Design Principles
- Make identification the center of gravity. The primary action should be obvious within seconds.
- Keep progression legible. Unlocks, sightings, and collection state should be easy to understand at a glance.
- Use game cues as emphasis, not as friction. Reward moments should heighten success without slowing normal use.
- Favor product clarity over decorative theming. Every label, control, and state should stay understandable on first use.
- Design for one-handed mobile use first, then expand outward to larger screens.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard accessibility for the primary flows. Preserve readable text sizing on mobile, visible focus states, and clear labels for controls. Motion should be brief and stateful rather than decorative, and the interface should remain understandable even when reward moments or imagery are not available.
